Most people see standing water in their yard and think, "That's annoying."

It is. But it's also expensive if you ignore it.

Here's what standing water does over time:

Foundation damage. Water pooling against your foundation causes the clay underneath to swell unevenly. That pushes your slab. Cracks in walls, doors sticking, floors going uneven — that's the early stage. Foundation repair in North Texas runs $5,000 to $15,000. I've seen higher.

Erosion. Water that doesn't drain properly erodes soil away from your foundation, your fence posts, your patio. Slow at first. Then you notice a gap under the fence. Or your patio slab tilting.

Mosquitoes. Standing water is where mosquitoes breed. One puddle that sits for a week can produce thousands of them. In North Texas summers, that turns your backyard into a no-go zone.

Mold and rot. Constant moisture against your house — especially around the foundation or crawl space — creates conditions for mold. Not just outside. That moisture finds its way in.

The thing is, a drainage fix costs a fraction of a foundation repair. We're talking about getting ahead of the problem instead of paying to fix the damage later.

Just something to think about next time you see water sitting in your yard after a storm.
