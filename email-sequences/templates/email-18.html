Fair question. I get this one a lot.

Yes, we dig trenches. That's how French drains work. There's no way around it — to put pipe and gravel in the ground, we have to open up the ground.

Here's what that actually looks like:

During the job: You'll see a trench about 12-18 inches wide and 12-18 inches deep running across part of your yard. Dirt gets piled to the side. It looks messy. I won't sugarcoat that.

Right after: We backfill the trench, grade it smooth, and clean up. The trench line will be visible — it's fresh dirt. It won't look like the rest of your yard yet.

Two to four weeks later: Grass starts growing back over the trench line. In North Texas, Bermuda grass fills in fast during growing season. Within a few weeks, you can barely tell we were there.

Two months later: You can't tell. Seriously. The trench line disappears. Your yard looks normal — except now water actually drains.

A few things that help:

  • We schedule the job during growing season whenever possible, so grass fills in faster
  • We clean up the job site before we leave. No piles of dirt, no debris
  • If there's sod that needs replacing, we'll talk about that upfront

The yard will look rough for a couple weeks. I won't pretend otherwise. But that's a couple weeks of cosmetic disruption to fix a problem that's been causing damage for years.

Worth the trade, in my opinion.
