Quick question: how often do your kids or grandkids play in the backyard during summer?

Now think about that puddle that sits for three or four days after every rain.

One puddle sitting for seven days can produce thousands of mosquitoes. Not a few. Thousands. In North Texas, that's not just itchy bites. West Nile virus is active in Tarrant and Parker County every single summer.

But mosquitoes aren't the only issue.

Standing water near your house creates moisture problems. Mold grows where you can't see it — against the foundation, in the crawl space, behind exterior walls. That musty smell in your house after a rain? That's not normal. That's a moisture problem.

And if you've got pets, standing water collects bacteria and runoff that isn't great for dogs who like to drink out of every puddle they find.

I know drainage isn't the most exciting thing to spend money on. Nobody puts "French drain" on their wish list. But it's one of those things that quietly makes your yard safer and more usable for your family.

If standing water hangs around your property after every storm, it's telling you something.
