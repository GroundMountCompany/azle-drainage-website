Here's something most homeowners around Azle don't know.

The soil under your yard isn't regular dirt. It's clay. Dense, heavy, North Texas clay that behaves nothing like the sandy soil you'd find an hour north of here.

When it rains, this clay swells up like a sponge. It holds water instead of letting it drain through. That's why your yard turns into a pond after a storm — the water has nowhere to go.

When it dries out, the clay shrinks and cracks. Those cracks run right up against your foundation. Over time, that swell-shrink cycle is what causes foundation movement, cracked walls, and doors that won't close right.

This matters because most drainage solutions you'll find online are designed for normal soil. The standard playbook — dig a trench, lay some pipe, fill it with gravel — doesn't hold up in clay. The clay migrates into the gravel, packs around the pipe, and within a couple years you're back to standing water.

Every system we build starts with one question: what's the clay doing on this specific property?

That's what makes drainage in North Texas different from drainage anywhere else.
