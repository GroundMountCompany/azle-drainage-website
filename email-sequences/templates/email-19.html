Drainage problems don't fix themselves. They get worse. Every storm that dumps water against your foundation, every week that standing water sits in your yard — the damage compounds.

Here's what another year of waiting looks like:

Foundation movement. Clay soil that stays saturated on one side of your house and dry on the other creates uneven pressure on your slab. That movement gets worse with every wet/dry cycle. One more year of that cycle = more movement = more expensive to fix later.

Erosion. Soil washing away from your foundation exposes more of the slab. That creates new water entry points. Each storm takes a little more soil with it.

Mosquito seasons. Each summer, those puddles breed thousands of mosquitoes. That's not a one-time thing. That's every year you wait.

Mold growth. Moisture against your house doesn't stop between rainstorms. It stays. Mold grows. Another year of moisture = another year of mold you can't see.

I'm not telling you this to rush you. I'm telling you because I've walked hundreds of properties where the homeowner said "I knew I should have done this three years ago."

Every single time.
