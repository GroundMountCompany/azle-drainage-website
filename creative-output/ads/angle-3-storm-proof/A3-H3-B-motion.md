## Animation Concept

This video tells one complete story: water falls from the sky, enters a drainage system, and exits to the street. The camera follows the water's journey from intake to outflow in a single continuous tracking shot. The visual proof is undeniable — the system works.

Beat 1 — THE ENEMY (0-1s): Camera starts tight on a roof edge during heavy rain — water cascades off shingles, pouring in a heavy stream down an aluminum downspout. The volume is aggressive. Dark storm clouds churn overhead. Thunder rumbles. This is a North Texas gully-washer, the kind that floods yards in minutes.

Beat 2 — THE SOLUTION (1-3s): Camera tilts down following the water as it exits the downspout and pours into a round green catch basin at the foundation corner. Water hits the basin grate with a splash. Camera then tracks laterally at ground level along the French drain trench — white gravel glistening wet, water flowing visibly through the channel away from the foundation. The bermuda grass on both sides is green and dry. The system is swallowing every drop.

Beat 3 — THE PROOF (3-5s): Camera continues tracking along the drain trench as it curves toward the street. Water exits the drain pipe at the curb, flowing clean and steady into the street gutter. Camera holds on the outflow — proof of function. Then slowly tilts up to reveal the full property in the background: dry foundation, green lawn, dark storm sky. Rain still pouring. The yard is storm-proof.
