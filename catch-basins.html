What Are Catch Basins?

Catch basins are underground drainage structures designed to collect surface water from multiple sources and direct it into your drainage system, preventing flooding in low-lying areas.

üíß

How Catch Basins Work

Catch basins feature a grated opening at ground level that collects surface water. The basin includes a sump area below to trap sediment and debris, while clean water flows through outlet pipes into your drainage system or storm sewer.

üè°

When You Need Catch Basins

Install catch basins in low spots where water accumulates, at the end of driveways, in parking areas, or anywhere surface water needs to be collected. They're essential for preventing flooding and protecting your property from water damage.

üî®

Installation Process

We excavate the catch basin location, install a pre-cast concrete or polymer basin with grate, connect outlet pipes to your drainage system, and backfill properly. The grate sits flush with the ground surface for easy water collection.

Catch Basins in North Texas

North Texas weather and soil conditions make catch basins especially valuable for property protection. Our area's unique challenges require properly designed and installed catch basin systems.

Flash Flooding Protection

North Texas experiences sudden, intense rainstorms that can dump several inches of rain in a short period. Catch basins provide immediate surface water collection, preventing flash flooding in vulnerable areas like driveways, patios, and low spots in your yard.

Clay Soil Management

Our expansive clay soil doesn't absorb water quickly, causing surface pooling and runoff. Catch basins efficiently collect this surface water before it can pool near your foundation or create erosion problems. The sump area also traps clay sediment, preventing drainage system clogs.

Multiple Water Sources

North Texas properties often have water coming from multiple directions‚Äîroof runoff, driveway drainage, neighbor's yard runoff, and street flow. Catch basins serve as collection hubs that consolidate these water sources into a single, manageable drainage system.

Debris Management

Our area's seasonal leaf fall, wind-blown debris, and dust require catch basins with proper sediment traps. We design systems that capture debris while allowing water to flow freely, and we provide maintenance services to keep them functioning properly year-round.

How deep should a catch basin be?
+

Most residential catch basins are 2-4 feet deep, with at least 12-18 inches of sump space below the outlet pipe to trap sediment. The exact depth depends on your drainage system's elevation and where the outlet pipe needs to connect.

Do I need a permit to install a catch basin?
+

Permit requirements vary by municipality in North Texas. Most residential catch basins that drain on your property don't require permits, but connections to storm sewers or work in easements may require approval. We handle all permit research and applications for you.

How often do catch basins need cleaning?
+

In North Texas, we recommend cleaning catch basins at least once per year, typically in spring after the leaf season. Properties with heavy debris, trees nearby, or high sediment should have them cleaned twice yearly to prevent clogs and maintain proper drainage.

Can you repair a broken or clogged catch basin?
+

Yes! We repair broken grates, replace damaged basins, clean severe clogs, fix cracked structures, and restore proper drainage. Many catch basin problems can be repaired without complete replacement, saving you money while restoring full function.
