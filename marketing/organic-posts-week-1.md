  FACEBOOK VERSION:

  People ask me all the time why their yard holds water when
  their neighbor's drains just fine.

  Nine times out of ten, the answer is clay.

  North Texas soil is some of the heaviest clay in the country.
  When it rains, that clay swells up tight -- water can't pass
  through it. It just sits there. Pools up. Creeps toward your
  foundation.

  Then summer hits, the clay shrinks, cracks open, and your
  foundation shifts. That's the cycle that causes real damage
  out here.

  A French drain doesn't fight the clay. It gives the water
  a path around it -- gravel, perforated pipe, and gravity
  doing the work.

  If your yard stays soggy 2-3 days after rain, that's not
  normal. That's clay doing what clay does. And it's fixable.
