  BRAND VISUAL IDENTITY

  Colors:
  ├── Primary: #115740 (deep green)
  ├── Accent: #f5a623 (warm orange)
  ├── Text: White on dark, black on light
  └── Avoid: Bright greens, neon, gradients

  Typography:
  ├── Headlines: Bold sans-serif, all caps
  │   or sentence case. No script fonts.
  ├── Body overlays: Clean sans-serif,
  │   high contrast for readability.
  └── Keep it simple. One font family max.

  Photo style:
  ├── Real job sites, real soil, real tools
  ├── Phone camera quality is fine -- and
  │   preferred for video. Overproduction
  │   kills trust.
  ├── Natural light whenever possible
  ├── Dirty boots, clay-stained hands, real
  │   work truck = credibility signals
  └── NO stock photos. Ever. People can tell.

  Bert on camera:
  ├── Work clothes, not a polo with a logo
  ├── On a job site or in/near his truck
  ├── Talking to camera like he's talking
  │   to a neighbor over a fence
  ├── No teleprompter. Bullet points at most.
  │   Natural pauses and "uh"s are fine.
  └── Confident, not salesy. Informative,
      not pushy.

  CREATIVE BRIEFS: REMAINING AD MATRIX ADS

  For all remaining ads not in the priority 5
  above, follow these format-specific guidelines:

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ALL STATIC IMAGE ADS (Format A)

  Dimensions: 1080x1080 (primary), 1080x1920
  (story placement)
  File format: JPG or PNG, < 5MB
  Text rule: Keep text under 20% of image area
  (Meta's recommendation for best delivery)

  Visual patterns by hook:
  ├── Direct (H1): Hero image of Bert or a
  │   finished job. Bold headline overlay.
  │   Clean, confident, uncluttered.
  ├── Question (H2): Split image or a single
  │   compelling photo that raises a question.
  │   Use "?" in the overlay sparingly.
  ├── Proof (H3): Before/after splits, data
  │   callouts, real job photos with results.
  │   Timestamps and dollar amounts are gold.
  └── Contrarian (H4): Bold text-heavy design.
      "Your landscaper can't fix this" style
      statements as the primary visual element.
      Photo supports the claim underneath.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ALL VIDEO ADS (Format B)

  Duration: 25-60 seconds (sweet spot: 30-40)
  Orientation: 9:16 primary, 1:1 secondary crop
  Resolution: 1080p minimum
  Audio: Bert's voice + natural sounds. No stock
  music unless it's very subtle and low.

  Structure template:
  0:00-0:03  Hook (matches the headline -- this
             is the scroll-stopper. Front-load
             the most surprising/specific claim)
  0:03-0:10  Setup (the problem or context)
  0:10-0:25  Body (proof, mechanism, story)
  0:25-0:35  Our approach (what we do different)
  0:35-0:40  CTA (text/call/form -- pick one)

  Production notes:
  ├── Shoot on an iPhone. Tripod optional.
  │   Handheld is fine and often better.
  ├── Landscape shots of job sites as B-roll
  │   between Bert's on-camera segments.
  ├── Show the work: trenches, pipe, gravel,
  │   water flowing. People watch process.
  ├── Captions/subtitles are MANDATORY.
  │   85% of Meta video is watched on mute.
  └── End card: Phone number + "Text us a photo"
      in brand green. Hold for 3 seconds.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ALL CAROUSEL ADS (Format C)

  Slides: 5-6 per carousel
  Dimensions: 1080x1080 per slide
  File format: JPG or PNG

  Structure template:
  Slide 1: Hook card (bold text, minimal image
           or strong single photo)
  Slide 2: The problem (photo + short text)
  Slide 3: The mechanism or proof (data, close-
           up, comparison)
  Slide 4: The fix (our install, our approach)
  Slide 5: The result (after photo, homeowner
           quote, dry yard)
  Slide 6: CTA card (phone number, "Text us a
           photo," or "Get a free assessment")

  Design notes:
  ├── Each slide must work standalone for
  │   people who don't swipe all the way.
  ├── Use consistent header bar across all
  │   slides (brand green, same font).
  ├── Arrow or "Swipe" indicator on slide 1
  │   to drive engagement.
  └── Slide 6 CTA should be unmissable:
      large phone number, clear action button.

  CREATIVE BRIEFS: ORGANIC POSTS

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 1: Clay Soil Carousel (Week 1)

  Slide 1: Bert's hand holding a chunk of North
  Texas clay. Wet, compacted, red-gray color.
  Text overlay: "This is why your yard won't
  drain." Shot outdoors, natural light.

  Slide 2: Side-by-side comparison. Left: clay
  soil sample (dense, wet). Right: sandy/loam
  soil with water visibly draining through it.
  Simple labels: "Your soil" / "Normal soil."

  Slide 3: French drain cross-section in an
  actual trench. Labels pointing to pipe,
  gravel, fabric, clay walls. Can be a real
  photo with arrows drawn on, or a whiteboard
  sketch. Real is better.

  Slide 4: Finished French drain in a local
  yard. Clean grade, green grass, no standing
  water. Text: "Water moves. Foundation stays
  put." Azle Drainage logo in corner.

  Slide 5 (optional): CTA card. "Text us a
  photo of your yard." Phone number large.
  Brand green background.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 2: Before/After Rain Day (Week 1)

  Two photos from the SAME property, same
  angle, similar conditions:

  Photo 1 (Before): Yard during/after rain.
  Standing water against foundation or fence.
  Include a visual anchor (tree, fence post,
  patio edge) that appears in both photos.
  "BEFORE" label, white text with dark shadow.

  Photo 2 (After): Same yard, same angle,
  during or after a comparable rain. Dry.
  Grass visible. Install visible if possible
  (catch basin, drain grate). "AFTER" label.

  Keep it dead simple. The photos do the work.
  No additional graphics needed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 3: Text Us a Photo CTA (Week 1)

  Option A (preferred): Phone screen showing
  a real text thread. Homeowner texted a photo
  of a flooded yard. Reply shows a casual
  diagnosis: "Yeah I can see the problem. Water's
  running off your neighbor's lot and pooling at
  that low spot. We can fix that. When's a good
  time for me to swing by?"

  Use a real screenshot with permission (blur
  name/number) or stage one on a real phone.
  NOT a mockup graphic. NOT a stock template.

  Option B: Bert in a yard holding his phone up
  toward camera, showing a drainage problem photo
  on screen. Work truck visible. Casual posture.
  "Text us a photo" text overlay.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 4: French Drain Reel (Week 2)

  Vertical video, 45-60 seconds. Shot on phone.

  Key shots (in order):
  1. Soggy yard or puddle (2-3 seconds)
  2. Bert talks to camera: "Let me show you"
  3. Trench being dug -- show the clay in the
     walls. "See that gray stuff? That's clay."
  4. Perforated pipe being placed. Show holes.
  5. Gravel being poured over pipe.
  6. Finished product. Clean yard.
  7. Bert to camera: "Text us a photo."

  Add subtitles. White text, dark background
  bar. Position in lower third.

  Raw footage is the goal. If it looks like a
  commercial, you've gone too far.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 5: Tear-Out Reveal Carousel (Week 2)

  Slide 1: The dig-up in progress. Old pipe
  exposed in clay, dirty, ugly. Bert holding
  the pipe or pointing at it. This is the
  attention-grabber.

  Slide 2: Close-up of the problems. Solid pipe
  (no holes), clay packed tight. Text labels:
  "Not perforated," "No gravel," "Zero slope."
  Keep labels clean and readable.

  Slide 3: New install going in. Perforated
  PVC on gravel bed, fabric visible, trench
  with visible slope. Label the components.

  Slide 4: Finished yard. "Same yard. Actually
  works this time." Text overlay. Logo and
  phone number.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  POST 6: Friday Weather/Personality (Week 2)

  Option A (preferred): Bert or crew at end of
  day. Job site or truck. Late afternoon light.
  Dirty boots, shovel against truck, maybe
  drinking water or looking at finished work.
  Casual, unposed. "End of a hard day" energy.

  Option B: View from inside the truck cab.
  Job site or dramatic sky visible through
  windshield. Dashboard, work gloves, coffee
  cup. Real working-truck interior.

  Option C: 15-20 second selfie video. Bert on
  site, talking about the weather and the work.
  No script. General vibe: "March in North
  Texas, am I right? Stay dry this weekend."

  This post is about PERSONALITY, not selling.
  The visual should make people feel like they
  know Bert. Warm, human, local.

  PHOTO/VIDEO CAPTURE CHECKLIST

  Keep this in the truck. Every job is content.

  On EVERY job:
  □ Before photo (same angle you'll use for
    after shot -- mark the spot)
  □ During photo (trench open, crew working)
  □ After photo (same angle as before)
  □ Close-up of soil (show the clay)

  On TEAR-OUT jobs:
  □ Failed pipe pulled from ground
  □ Cross-section of failed pipe (cut it open)
  □ Close-up of clay packed in/around pipe
  □ New install going in for contrast
  □ 30-second Bert walkthrough video

  On RAIN DAYS:
  □ Visit 2-3 completed jobs
  □ Film water flowing through the system
  □ Film the discharge point working
  □ 15-second clip of each for ad and organic

  STORAGE:
  ├── Create a "Content" album on phone
  ├── Tag by job location and date
  ├── Back up to Google Drive weekly
  └── Best footage goes straight to the
      marketing folder for ad production
