  7-Dimension Copy Analysis
  Azle Drainage Solutions
  Generated Feb 20, 2026

  Scoring Rubric (1-10 each):
  ├── Clarity:     How immediately clear is the
  │                message? Can someone get it in
  │                3 seconds?
  ├── Specificity: How concrete? Numbers, names,
  │                places vs. vague generalities.
  ├── Voice:       How well does it sound like
  │                Bert talking to a neighbor?
  ├── Desire:      How strongly does it trigger
  │                the dream outcome?
  ├── Proof:       How much evidence/credibility
  │                is present?
  ├── Urgency:     How compelling is the reason
  │                to act now?
  └── Flow:        How well does copy move from
                   hook to body to CTA?

  Total possible: 70 per ad

  PATTERNS OBSERVED

  Hook dominance:
  ├── Proof hooks hold 10 of 15 finalist
  │   spots. In a Late Stage 2 market
  │   approaching Stage 3, proof beats
  │   claims. This tracks with Schwartz.
  └── Contrarian hooks hold 4 of 15.
      Strong for engagement/shares but
      score lower on Desire and Proof.

  Format dominance:
  ├── Video holds 9 of 15 finalist spots.
  │   On-camera Bert with physical evidence
  │   is the highest-performing combination.
  ├── Static holds 4 spots. Fastest to
  │   produce and can match video on
  │   conversion rate.
  └── Carousel holds 2 spots. Best for
      educational/swipe engagement but
      needs stronger visual assets.

  Scoring gaps:
  ├── Urgency is the weakest dimension
  │   across all 60 ads (avg ~6.3). None
  │   of the angles naturally create time
  │   pressure. Consider adding seasonal
  │   callouts ("spring storms are 6 weeks
  │   out") to boost this.
  └── Desire scores higher when the ad
      includes a specific "after" result
      (homeowner quote, dry yard, dollar
      savings) vs. just attacking the
      problem.
