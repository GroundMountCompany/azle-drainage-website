üîß Technical Questions

A French drain is an underground trench filled with gravel and a perforated pipe that collects and redirects subsurface water. It handles water throughout the soil. A catch basin is a surface drain with a grated top that captures water in one spot and channels it through solid pipes to a drainage point. French drains prevent water accumulation; catch basins remove water that's already pooled. Most effective systems use both.

Warning signs include: water pooling in your yard 24+ hours after rain, muddy spots that never dry, water against your foundation, damp basement or crawl space, erosion near downspouts, standing water creating mosquito problems, difficulty growing grass in soggy areas, or cracks in your foundation accompanied by drainage issues. If you see any of these around your Azle property, text us photos for an assessment.

Clay soil is our specialty‚Äîit's what we deal with on almost every Azle-area project. North Texas clay expands when wet (causing foundation movement) and creates a nearly impermeable layer that prevents water absorption. We design drainage systems specifically for clay conditions: deeper trenches, proper gravel selection, correct pipe placement, and adequate slope. Our solutions work WITH clay soil characteristics instead of fighting them.
