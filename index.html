What's the difference between a French drain and a catch basin?
+

A French drain is an underground trench filled with gravel and a perforated pipe that redirects water away from problem areas. A catch basin is a surface drain that collects water in one spot and channels it through underground pipes. French drains handle subsurface water, while catch basins manage surface water. Many properties in Azle need both for complete drainage solutions.
